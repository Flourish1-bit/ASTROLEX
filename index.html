Outer Space Treaty (1967)

114 parties · Cornerstone of space law — Article I: free exploration; Art II: no national appropriation; Art III: compliance with UN Charter; Art IV: ban on WMD in orbit; Art VI: state responsibility for commercial actors; Art VII: liability; Art VIII: jurisdiction over objects; Art IX: harmful interference & consultation; Art X-XV: cooperation, visits to space stations, dispute settlement.

  • Over 100+ national space laws derived from OST principles.
  • Interpretive debates: "peaceful purposes" allows military use not aggressive.

Moon Agreement (1979)

Common Heritage of Mankind (CHM) — Article 11: Moon and its natural resources are common heritage. Requires international regime for resource exploitation. Only 18 ratifications, but influential for seabed mining and lunar governance.

  • Art 6: freedom of scientific investigation; Art 7: protection of lunar environment; Art 8: demilitarization.
  • Resurgence in lunar policy debates (2025-2030).

Liability & Registration

Liability Convention (1972): Absolute liability for surface damage (Earth), fault liability in space. Joint & several liability for joint launches. Registration Convention (1976): mandatory UN registry of space objects, orbital parameters, function.

  • Cosmos 954 (1978) remains only settled liability claim ($3M CAD).
  • Registration gaps: military satellites often omit details.

UNCOPUOS Principles

Remote Sensing (1986): nondiscriminatory access; Nuclear Power Sources (1992): safety assessments; Debris Mitigation (2007): 25-year disposal rule; Long-Term Sustainability (2019): 21 guidelines including space traffic coordination.

Hague Building Blocks (2019): 11 principles for space resources: transparency, prior consultation, benefit-sharing, registry.

Critical Legal Theories

Jus Cogens: non-appropriation & peaceful purposes as peremptory norms. Postcolonial critique: space law as hegemonic tool; need for Global South inclusion. Feminist jurisprudence: care ethics, reproductive rights in space. Cosmopolitan democracy: World Space Organization proposal. Ecological materialism: planetary boundaries extended to celestial bodies.

Space Economy & Property

Global space economy: $630B (2023) → $1.8T by 2035. Legal clash: US/Luxembourg national laws allow asteroid resource rights vs. Moon Agreement's CHM. Hague Working Group seeks compromise via international authorization mechanism.

  • Artemis Accords (29 signatories): "safety zones" and resource extraction rights.

Mega-Constellations

Starlink (6k+ sats), OneWeb, Kuiper — orbital congestion, light pollution, collision risk. ITU WRC-27 agenda on orbital carrying capacity. Legal proposals: orbital slot fees, binding debris mitigation, spectrum auction.

Space Weaponization

No explicit ban on conventional ASATs. 2021 Russian ASAT created debris threatening ISS. UN resolution 77/41 (2022) moratorium on destructive tests. Woomera Manual (2022) applies IHL to space: distinction, proportionality, precautions.

Debris & Environment

~36,500 debris >10cm, 1 million >1cm. Kessler Syndrome risk. Zero Debris Charter (2023) by ESA targets 2030. Legal hurdles: ADR requires consent (Art VIII OST). Draft International Code of Conduct for space activities.

Comprehensive Space Law Encyclopedia

United States

Commercial Space Launch Competitiveness Act (2015) — property rights in asteroid resources. FCC licensing for satellites. Artemis Accords leader. NASA's Moon to Mars legal framework. One Small Step Act protects Apollo sites.

European Union

Luxembourg Space Law (2017) — mining rights. France: Space Operations Act (2008). Germany: national space law draft. EU Space Programme (2021) for Galileo, Copernicus. Supports Hague Building Blocks.

China & Russia

China: National Space Law (draft) — peaceful use but military integration. International Lunar Research Station (ILRS) with Russia as Artemis alternative. Russia: Law on Space Activities (1993) — opposes unilateral resource claims.
